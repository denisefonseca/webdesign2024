Paradise

The Maldives are an exotic tropical paradise located off the southern tips of Sri Lanka and India and are a group of around 1190 coral islands consisting of 26 Atoll formations. Scattered across the equator in the middle of the Indian Ocean, the gem like islands of the Maldives depict the rare vision of a tropical paradise with their sparkling white powder soft palm fringed beaches, turquoise lagoons, clear warm waters and vibrant coral reefs, providing some of the best diving locations in the world.

With each individual resort set on its own island this is the perfect place for the sun lover, the diver, the honeymooners and even families who desire peace and tranquility in its deepest meaning. With comprehensive environmental policies in each resort it ensures the natural eco systems of the islands stay just that, natural and unspoilt.

International flights arrive at the capital city of Male where you will then pick up your domestic seaplane or speedboat transfer to your chosen island resort. Seaplane transfers are a rare treat that add an extra layer to your experience of the Maldives. Flying over the islands at low heights you can see the shapes of reefs and colours of shallow waters around every island!

With 26 atolls making up the archipelago, there are lots of places to discover on a holiday to the Maldives. With luxurious accommodations and sea views for miles, the only thing you have to think about is where to go. Will it be the Meemu atoll, Vilamendhoo island, or somewhere completely different?
Comprising of tiny little islands, it’s not surprising that many of the biggest attractions in the Maldives are water-based. Snorkelling and scuba diving are popular watersports here, along with swimming and simply relaxing by the beach.

But for those with a real love for adventure, shark diving can deliver a whole new level of thrill.

You’ll find that there are plenty of boat trips in the Maldives, transporting travellers between the atolls as they search for new adventures. If you’re looking for places to visit, try the capital of Male where you can tour the local mosques. And while you’re there, be sure to take a trip to the Ari atolls which are bursting with scuba diving sites. While each atoll is different, you’ll find things to be quieter towards the north, and more developed towards the south.
Holidays to the Maldives are a favourite of newlyweds, and that’s because of the luxurious places to stay that can be found across the atolls. Due to the lack of land, many top accommodations are positioned on stilts, jutting out over the water for amazing views of the Indian Ocean. Pair this with four-poster beds and tables for two, and you’ve got everything you need for a hot honeymoon destination.
